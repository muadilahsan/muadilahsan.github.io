Trust, Incentives, and Coordination in Local Service Delivery

Job market paper

Draft Slides

This project studies how small administrative frictions reshape performance when frontline workers rely on trust, repeated interaction, and informal coordination to deliver public services. Combining administrative records with field evidence, I show that modest changes in reporting and monitoring can improve reliability without suppressing local discretion.

Networks, Hiring, and Informal Gatekeeping

with A. Collaborator

Draft

We examine how referral-based hiring can expand information while also concentrating opportunity within already connected groups. Using matched employer-worker data and a lightweight field intervention, the paper traces when networks reduce search costs and when they reinforce exclusion.

Credit, Reputation, and Repeat Exchange in Urban Markets

with B. Collaborator and C. Collaborator

Draft Data

This paper looks at deferred payment arrangements among small businesses and regular customers. We show how everyday credit operates as both a financial tool and a social signal, shaping who returns, who repays, and who gets offered flexibility in the first place.
